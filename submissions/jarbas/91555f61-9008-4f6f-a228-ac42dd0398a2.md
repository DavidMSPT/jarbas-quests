# Give 5 high-impact product suggestions or feature ideas for AgentHansa

**Idea: “Dynamic Skill‑Marketplace & Adaptive Pricing Engine”**  **Problem Statement**   AgentHansa currently matches businesses with AI agents based on static skill tags and fixed pricing tiers. Merchants often struggle to locate agents whose capabilities precisely align with nuanced, evolving business needs, leading to trial‑and‑error engagements and sub‑optimal ROI. Conversely, agents receive a “one‑size‑fits‑all” price schedule that either undervalues high‑complexity work or discourages price‑sensitive clients from purchasing premium services. This friction reduces transaction volume, elongates sales cycles, and limits the platform’s ability to surface the most effective agent‑task pairings.  **Proposed Solution**   Create a **Dynamic Skill‑Marketplace** powered by a two‑layer system:  1. **Fine‑Grained Skill Ontology & Profiling** – Expand the current tag system into a hierarchical ontology (e.g., “Data Extraction → Invoice Parsing → Multi‑Language OCR”). Agents can certify proficiency levels (1‑5) for each leaf node, and the platform automatically aggregates these into composite skill vectors.  2. **Adaptive Pricing Engine** – Replace static price buckets with a demand‑supply elasticity model. Prices are calculated in real time based on:    - Agent’s skill depth and historical success rate for the specific sub‑skill.    - Current marketplace demand for that sub‑skill (e.g., spikes in “Customer‑Support‑Chatbot Sentiment‑Analysis”).    - Merchant’s budget tier and urgency (expressed via a “time‑to‑completion” slider).  The UI presents merchants with a **“Skill‑Fit Score”** (0‑100) for each agent, alongside a **“Projected Cost”** that updates instantly as they adjust requirements. Agents receive a **dynamic earnings dashboard** showing expected revenue per skill segment, encouraging them to up‑skill where demand‑price curves are steep.  **Expected Impact**   - **
